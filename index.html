MO DEHESTANI, PhD

Computational Biologist & Data Scientist

I am a computational biologist and genomic data scientist with a strong focus on multimodal omics integration and machine learning to uncover the molecular underpinnings of complex diseases. By leveraging my deep domain knowledge in disease molecular biology, advanced computational methods, and proficiency in programming, I strive to bridge the gap between intricate biological datasets and clinical outcomes, facilitating the identification of druggable targets and the generation of actionable insights. My core expertise spans:

  • - Single-cell omics and multiomics analysis
  • - Machine learning for predictive modeling
  • - Statistical genomics (exploratory GWAS & causality-driven post-GWAS)
  • - Large-scale genomic analysis and cohort stratification by *omics data
  • - Data wrangling, cleaning, visualization, and application of FAIR principles
  • - Co-supervising and leading research projects in statistical genomics and computational biology
  • - Collaborating with cross-functional teams

Currently, I am advancing our understanding of neurodegenerative diseases as part of my work at the DZNE (German Center for Neurodegenerative Diseases), using data-driven approaches to generate novel insights into these challenging conditions.
